Key Purchases

  • Tip 1 for Key Purchases: A safe car seat is non-negotiable.
  • Tip 2 for Key Purchases: Consider a versatile pushchair system.
  • Tip 3 for Key Purchases: You'll need more muslins than you think!

Hospital Bag

  • Pack for yourself: comfy clothes, toiletries, snacks, phone charger.
  • Pack for baby: a few outfits, nappies, wipes, a blanket.
  • Don't forget your maternity notes!

Nursery

  • Ensure a safe sleeping space (cot or Moses basket).
  • Good storage solutions for nappies, clothes, and toys.
  • A comfortable feeding chair can be a lifesaver.

Signs of Labour

  • Contractions becoming regular, stronger, and closer together.
  • A "show" (mucus plug dislodging).
  • Waters breaking (can be a gush or a trickle).

Birth Pain Relief Menu

  • Non-medical: breathing techniques, movement, water birth, TENS machine.
  • Medical: gas and air (Entonox), pethidine, epidural.
  • Discuss options with your midwife beforehand.

Interventions List

  • Induction of labour.
  • Assisted delivery (forceps or ventouse).
  • Caesarean section.
  • Always ask for benefits, risks, and alternatives (BRAIN).

Nappy Changing

  • Have everything ready before you start: clean nappy, wipes, cream.
  • Always wipe front to back for girls.
  • Frequent changes help prevent nappy rash.

Bathing

  • Ensure water is warm, not hot (test with elbow or thermometer).
  • Support baby's head and neck securely.
  • Have a warm towel ready. You don't need to bathe a newborn daily.

Feeding

  • Breastfeeding: ensure a good latch, feed on demand. Seek support if needed.
  • Bottle feeding: follow formula instructions carefully, sterilise equipment.
  • Look for feeding cues: rooting, sucking fists, restlessness.

Sleeping

  • Place baby on their back to sleep in a clear cot or Moses basket.
  • Keep baby's head uncovered and room temperature comfortable (16-20°C).
  • Avoid smoking around your baby.

CPR (Cardiopulmonary Resuscitation) for Infants

  • If an infant is unresponsive and not breathing normally, call emergency services immediately.
  • Basic infant CPR involves cycles of 30 chest compressions and 2 rescue breaths.
  • Crucial: Attend a certified infant first aid course to learn these life-saving skills properly.

Choking - Infant First Aid

  • If an infant is choking (unable to cry or cough effectively), act immediately.
  • Deliver up to 5 back blows, followed by up to 5 chest thrusts if needed.
  • Crucial: Attend a certified infant first aid course to learn how to respond to choking.

First Few Days at Home

  • Rest when baby rests. Don't try to do too much.
  • Accept help from friends and family.
  • Monitor baby's feeding, wet/dirty nappies, and general well-being. Contact your midwife or GP if concerned.
